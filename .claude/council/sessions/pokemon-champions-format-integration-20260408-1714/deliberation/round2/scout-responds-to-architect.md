## Scout Response to Architect

**Their position:** Add `generation` and `calc_backend` fields to FormatConfig plus a `game` column on dex tables — treating Champions as a parallel format track that routes to a different backend.

**My response:** Modify

**Reasoning:** Architect's `generation`/`calc_backend` abstraction is the right direction but undershoots the scope of the stat system divergence. The confirmed Champions stat formula is `stat = floor((2 * Base) * Level/100) + Level + 10 + SP`, where SP is a direct integer addition (1 SP = 1 stat point at Lv50) with no IVs at all — no IV slot, no EV/4 division, no nature modifier on the stat formula (unclear if natures survive into Champions). This isn't a backend-routing problem; the `spreads` table schema with EV columns per stat literally cannot represent Champions spreads, and any code that reads those columns and passes them into the Gen 9 formula will silently compute wrong damage rolls. On the Porygon Labs integration question: their damage calc confirms it accepts Stat Points natively, but they have **no public API** — rate-limited internal endpoints exist but are undocumented and could break or block us without notice. This makes them unsuitable as a dependency; they're a reference implementation to validate our own Python calc against, not an integration target. Champions Lab is the same story — simulation data with no API. The practical path for damage calculation is to write a thin Python Champions calc (the formula is *simpler* than Gen 9, not harder: remove the IV term, replace EV/4 with SP directly) rather than depend on undocumented third-party APIs. Architect needs to know that `calc_backend` as a FormatConfig field is necessary but not sufficient — the database schema for spreads/team_pokemon also needs a `game`-aware storage model, and that's a schema migration question that belongs in Architect's domain, not a runtime routing flag.
